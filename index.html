What is KALPANA?

"Nothing is impossible for women, if they have a strong will". These were the words sent by Kalpana Chawla to all Indians, especially young girls. The name of this event is in rememberance of this woman who taught girls to dream of wings. She was the first woman of Indian origin to travel into space on NASA’s Space Shuttle mission, Columbia. Although we lost her in a tragic Space Shuttle disaster, she still remains an epitome of inspiration and motivation for all the men and women around the world and especially for the aspiring youth of India. So this event is an opportunity for all the intellect heads out there seeking for opportunities to prove that they got that will inside them.

The Women In Engineering [WIE] Chapter at IEEE PESIT South Student Branch presents to you a one day event which aims to bring Technology and Humanitarianism under one roof. This event consists of two main facets:-

Technology:
This facet of Kalpana consists of engineering students from various colleges across Karnataka to showcase their projects which solve real world problems currently existing in the society today. Check out our list of domains here. A stringent selection process will shortlist the best ideas/teams who can participate at this project exhibition. Since we are also supporting more women to enter into the field of Science, there will be a criteria of having at least 1 female member per team. Ample amount of time will be given to the shortlisted teams to prepare their projects for the same.

Humanitarianism :
Around 47 million young men and women around the world drop out of school by 10th grade. With such advancements in technology and awareness among people, this ratio can not be left unnoticed. So this facet of the event is aimed at inviting students from underdeveloped government schools across Bangalore to provide them with a talk delivered by appropriate speakers regarding their career and future. This is done in order to encourage and motivate them to complete their formal education even after high school. We believe that having events like this, would play a key role in incentivising the future of India. [This will be a private early morning session conducted by the branch before the competition formally begins].

We at PESIT South Student Branch trust that we have a moral responsibility as future engineers to give back to the community we live in. We hope that you believe in the same spirit and participate in Kalpana, 2018.

Rules

General Rules

  1. The team must select a domain from the ones provided by the organizers according to their projects. A domain can have many problem statements, to approach a particular problem is up to the team. For teams which can not find their domain in the list specified can register their idea under "Other" section.
  2. The solution to the problem cannot be a preexisting one. The team must think of a method to develop on the current solutions and bring forward a better one, or think of a completely new solution. Any kind of plagiarism content more than 30% leads to a negative score.
  3. The solution to a problem cannot be a social experiment. It must be a technical solution with proper scientific approach.
  4. The idea must be feasible, i.e., it should have the potential to be easily implemented in, and accepted by society.
  5. The idea should be focused at implementation in “Underdeveloped” and “Developing” nations.
  6. The team must keep in mind the cost of implementing the idea with the conditions mentioned above, and must keep an estimate ready.
  7. Synopsis must be submitted in the format described here.
  8. Any type of malpractices or harassment will not be entertained and will lead to direct disqualification of the team.

Team Specifications

To be eligible to participate in Kalpana 2018, a team must satisfy the following:-

  1. A team is to be composed of 2 to 3 members.
  2. At least one member of the team must be a woman. Failing to have any female member on the event day shall not be accepted and the team will be disqualified, disregarding the rank on the synopsis review.
  3. All members of a team should be students in any Undergraduate program in science or engineering in Karnataka (IEEE Bangalore Section geographical region).
  4. The team can have members from more than one institution in a city.
  5. The idea must satisfy the general requirements discussed above.
  6. The idea must be unique to a team. Reviewers shall check any possibilities of plagiarism for all entries.
  7. The team can have a mentor, who is a faculty from their institution. Presence of a mentor shall not affect the eligibility of a team and will not influence the evaluation of a synopsis in any way.
